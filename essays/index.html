Before the First Entity
Every ontology starts with something that already exists. But what was there before the first thing? This essay traces a structural convergence between Nagarjuna, Badiou, and Turing on the undifferentiated ground that precedes every act of differentiation.

Reality Is a Tangle
Similarity, identity, and knowledge are three words for the same puzzle: how does sameness coexist with difference? This essay argues that a topological theory of things -- where reality is a relational tangle read through structured coils -- dissolves all three at once, without invoking hidden essences.

The First Ontologist
Every ontology begins by listing kinds of things and kinds of relations. Nagarjuna questioned this move in the second century. This essay traces his argument through Western philosophy and category theory to the single-relation ontology that takes dependent origination seriously as a data structure.
1927 words
There Is No Meta-Room
How should we make decisions when we disagree about the rules for making decisions? This essay argues that no meta-framework exists to adjudicate between incompatible systems of truth, and that this structural absence is not a defect but the condition for pluralism.
